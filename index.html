First steps — do these immediately
Leave if your home is unsafe
Do not stay in rising water to save belongings. Flood water can contain sewage, chemicals, sharp debris, mold, and bacteria. If water got near outlets or appliances, have an electrician inspect before plugging anything back in.
Report damage to DEM/MEMA
Before federal funding is released to the state, DEM/MEMA must show local need. If your home or business was damaged, report it at the links provided in the Resources section — this directly unlocks more resources for Hawaiʻi.
Post your needs publicly
Post what you need at your aligned list linked in the Resources tab so community members and volunteers can help.
Day 1 — organization
Get a multi-sleeve file folder and hardbound notebook
You will carry this notebook with you for the next year. Trust it is worth its weight in gold — write down every call, name, and number in it.
Set up a dedicated Gmail address for disaster communications
Use this only for disaster-related emails so you aren't searching through your personal inbox. Add it to your phone. This way you can choose when you're ready to look at those emails.
Create a phone album for disaster documents
Save all damage photos, receipts, screenshots, claim numbers, adjuster business cards, and contractor estimates there. One place for everything.
Day 1 — document damage before cleaning
Take a full walkthrough video — say the date out loud
Film every room before cleaning: bathrooms, kitchens, walls, flooring, furniture, electronics, artwork, storage areas, tools, business inventory. Open every closet. Then take close-up photos too.
Photograph every valuable item and appliance
If you have contents coverage, this matters a lot. Capture furniture in each room, electronics, tools, business equipment, appliances, anything high-value, and the water line in each room and outside.
Photograph appliance model and serial number tags
These are often exactly what insurers need. Where to find them:
  • Refrigerator/freezer: usually inside, upper left area
  • Dishwasher: inside edge of door, side of door, or frame when opened
  • Stove/oven: inside oven door, side of door frame, inside bottom drawer
  • Washer: under the lid or on the frame
  • Dryer: inside door, on door frame, or back panel
Insurance — if you have coverage
Call your insurance company and open the claim
Do this as soon as you safely can. Ask for your claim number, adjuster contact, deadlines, what temporary repairs are allowed, and whether hotel, food, mileage, supplies, storage, or business interruption may be covered. Write every name down.
Leave evidence if you must clean before the adjuster arrives
If you bleach, power wash, or begin remediation, try to leave at least one visible flood line or clear evidence inside and outside — otherwise it becomes harder to prove the level of flooding. Move fast on drying, but don't destroy your evidence.
Start drying immediately once water recedes
You have a very short window before mold starts. Get air moving with fans, dehumidifiers, wet vacs, and ventilation as soon as it is safe. If drywall is wet, the sooner drying and assessment starts, the better.
Ongoing — every day
Keep a daily disaster journal
Write down who you called, who called back, claim number, what was damaged, what you bought, what you cleaned, where you stayed, what you were told, miles driven, and who told you to do what. It all blurs together fast.
Save receipts for everything
Hotel, meals, clothing, pet supplies, cleaning supplies, bins, tarps, fans, wet vac, dehumidifier, gas, mileage, laundry, temporary repairs, storage, replacement essentials, business supplies, equipment rentals. Put paper receipts in a gallon ziplock and photograph each one.
Set up ways to accept donations if you need help
Venmo, PayPal, Square, Cash App, and GoFundMe. Keep your GoFundMe description vague (e.g., "Storm Recovery") rather than specific — FEMA may call it a duplication of benefits if you received money for something they also cover.

What is this app?

The Disaster Recovery Companion is a free tool that lives at a website link. There's nothing to download or install — you just open it in your phone's browser like any other webpage.

Where is my data saved?

When you fill in your information, check off steps, or write in your journal, everything saves automatically to your phone's browser. Think of it like a notepad that remembers what you wrote the last time you opened it — as long as you're using the same phone and the same browser (like Safari or Chrome).

What if I switch phones, lose my phone, or want to use my laptop too?

This is where the two backup options come in:

Save Code — found in the My Info tab. Tap "Generate save code" and it creates a long string of letters and numbers that holds all your information. Text it to yourself, write it in your notebook, or email it to yourself. On any new device, open the app, go to My Info, paste the code, and everything comes back.
Google Drive Sync — the second tab in this app. If you sign in with a Google account, your information, checklist progress, and journal entries save to your Google Drive — like a filing cabinet in the cloud that you can open from any device. Your damage photos save to a folder in your Drive called "Disaster Recovery — Photos" which you can also share directly with your insurance adjuster.
Is my information private?

Yes. Nothing is sent to anyone else. Your data either stays on your phone or goes straight to your own personal Google Drive — nobody else can see it.

What about my photos?

Photos you add in the Photo Log tab stay on your phone's browser session. To make sure you don't lose them, tap "Upload photos to Google Drive" in the Photo Log tab — this sends each photo to your Drive folder one at a time. We also recommend keeping photos saved to your phone's camera roll as a second backup.

What if I clear my browser or get a new phone?

If you haven't used the Save Code or Google Drive sync, your data could be lost. That's why we strongly recommend doing one of those two things as soon as you start using the app — especially the Google Drive sync, since it's the most automatic.

The simple version: Open the app → sign into Google Drive on the Drive Sync tab → fill in your info → everything backs up automatically. If you ever switch devices, sign in with the same Google account and tap "Load from Google Drive" to get everything back.
